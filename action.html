Village Space

Village Space involves real life stories of peoples and their struggles to overcome injustice in their communities. Through the medium of interactive dramatised storytelling the audience is taken on a journey into the lives of peoples and their communities in 'majority/developing world' countries.

The use of scripted dialogue, song, dance, movement, music, visuals and simple props, taps into the audiences' senses, impacting on the heart, mind and emotions. The audience readily empathises with the characters' feelings of fear, anger, sadness and frustration, as well as determination and hope. Empathy is the beginning of the participants' motivation to work towards a just world.

The Village Space program is a great educational tool and can be a springboard into specific Key Learning Areas, especially Religious Education and Society and Environment. It provides inspiration for Social Justice Action within the school.

Australian Catholic Migrant and Refugee Office

The 2013 Migrant and Refugee Kit provides information and statistics as well as personal stories and reflection resources. The kit outlines the Catholic Church's stance on mandatory detention and identifies many struggles that migrants and refugees face.
