What do we stand for?

The Australian Coalition to End Immigration Detention of Children or End Child Detention Australia believes that every child should be treated in full accordance with international law regarding child rights. Giving children and families seeking asylum a platform to be heard, by decision makers and the broader public, is essential to protecting their rights.
The Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC), 1989 guides the position of this coalition.
Australia is a signatory to CRC and is therefore responsible for protecting the rights of all children, including those seeking asylum.
Determining a child's refugee status is an immigration issue. How that child is treated in the meantime is a child rights issue.


The values of this coalition are that;
  1. Immigration detention is never in the best interest of the child*
  2. Offshore processing should not be part of Australia's asylum policy. Children should be moved to the mainland as a matter of priority.
  3. Every child deserves access to education and healthcare.
  4. The family unit is essential and must be protected in a community setting.
  5. All people have the right to seek asylum.
* This is particularly the case with Australia's current practice of detention in secure facilities for an indefinite time period and in remote, even offshore locations.

What's the problem?

Alternative Places of Detention (APODs) are unsuitable for children. This has been confirmed by organisations such as Amnesty, ChilOut and the Australian Human Rights Commission. The child's experience is no different within an APOD to that of a "regular" detention centre, essentially their freedom is restricted in a similar way.

Agencies such as the Refugee Council of Australia, Uniting Justice Australia, Amnesty International Australia and the Australian Human Rights Commission have all found that tough policies, commonly referred to as 'policies of deterrence' - simply don't work. People have and always will flee situations of persecution and violence. Their numbers will fluctuate as conflicts escalate or ease.

Detention causes unrest, misery and depression. The effects last long after they are released and it is something that we are directly responsible for.
